## Scenario A: The Bootstrapped Bengaluru Founder (Pre-Revenue)

**Profile:**
- Age: 27 | City: Bengaluru | Single | No dependents
- Left ₹18L/year product manager job 6 months ago
- Startup salary: ₹0 (fully bootstrapped, living off savings)
- Monthly personal burn: ₹42,000
  - Rent: ₹18,000 | Food: ₹8,000 | Transport: ₹4,000 | Subscriptions/Tools: ₹6,000 | Misc: ₹6,000
- Cash savings: ₹9,50,000 (in savings account + 1 FD)
- Prior EPF balance: ₹2,20,000 (not yet withdrawn)
- Equity: 80% of company (fully owned, bootstrapped)
- Existing investments: ₹0 (stopped all SIPs when quit)

**Founder Runway Calculation:**
```
Personal Runway = ₹9,50,000 ÷ ₹42,000/month = 22.6 months
```
> ✅ Green at 22 months. But NO income means zero tax-saving investments and zero retirement compounding.

**Key Issues:**
1. EPF ₹2.2L sitting idle — should roll into PPF or NPS (not withdraw; 10% TDS + taxable if < 5 yrs service)
2. No health insurance (startup's group cover lapsed on quitting)
3. All savings in savings account earning ~3.5% — cash drag vs inflation

**Recommended Actions:**
1. **Week 1**: Buy individual health insurance (₹10L floater ~₹8,500/yr from Niva Bupa or Care Health)
2. **Week 2**: Move ₹2,52,000 (6 months burn) into liquid mutual fund as emergency fund ring-fence
3. **Month 1**: Open PPF account — deposit ₹500 to activate; plan ₹1.5L lump sum before 5 April each FY
4. **Month 2**: Open NPS Tier 1 on eNPS with ₹1,000 activation; plan ₹50,000/yr contribution for 80CCD(1B)
5. **Month 3**: Begin ₹5,000/month SIP in Nifty 50 index fund (direct plan) — resume compounding
6. **Tax regime**: New regime — no salary, so no deductions relevant; file ITR-1 with zero/minimal income

**Advance Tax**: Not applicable if total tax liability < ₹10,000/yr. File ITR by 31 July.

## Scenario B: The Funded Startup CEO, Mumbai (Seed Stage)

**Profile:**
- Age: 33 | City: Mumbai | Married, 1 child (age 3)
- Company raised ₹3Cr seed round 8 months ago
- Founder salary: ₹1,20,000/month (₹14.4L/year CTC)
- Monthly take-home (after TDS @ 10%): ~₹1,08,000
- Monthly personal burn: ₹98,000
  - Rent: ₹55,000 | EMI (car loan): ₹8,500 | Childcare: ₹12,000 | Food: ₹10,000 | Insurance: ₹3,500 | Misc: ₹9,000
- Monthly surplus: ~₹10,000
- Cash savings: ₹4,80,000
- Equity: 35% (diluted from 60% post seed; 4-year vesting, 12-month cliff — cliff completed)
- Existing investments: ₹1,40,000 in PPF (started 4 years ago), ₹80,000 in ELSS (stopped SIP when bootstrapping)
- EMI-to-income ratio: ₹8,500 ÷ ₹1,08,000 = 7.9% ✅

**Monthly Cash Flow Table:**
| Category | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Take-home salary | +₹1,08,000 |
| Rent | -₹55,000 |
| Car loan EMI | -₹8,500 |
| Childcare | -₹12,000 |
| Food & groceries | -₹10,000 |
| Insurance premiums | -₹3,500 |
| Subscriptions/misc | -₹9,000 |
| **Net Monthly Surplus** | **+₹10,000** |

**Founder Runway:**
```
Runway = ₹4,80,000 ÷ (₹98,000 − ₹1,08,000)
Income > Burn → Runway is technically infinite, but emergency fund is only ~4.9 months
Target emergency fund: ₹98,000 × 6 = ₹5,88,000 → Gap: ₹1,08,000
```
> ⚠️ Yellow: Income covers burn, but emergency fund is ₹1.08L short of 6-month target.

**Tax Analysis (Old vs New Regime on ₹14.4L gross):**

Old Regime Deductions:
| Deduction | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Standard Deduction | ₹50,000 |
| HRA (Mumbai — ~40% of basic, say basic = ₹60,000) | ₹36,000–₹72,000 (complex calc) |
| 80C (PPF ₹1.5L — currently contributing ₹35,000/yr; top up to ₹1.5L) | ₹1,50,000 |
| 80D (self + family health insurance) | ₹25,000 |
| 80CCD(1B) NPS (not yet contributing — opportunity!) | ₹0 (₹50,000 available) |
| **Estimated total deductions** | **~₹2.5–3L** |

> Recommendation: **Old Regime** (total deductions ~₹3L+ with HRA; saves significant tax vs new regime on ₹14.4L)
> Switch to new regime only if HRA exemption is not applicable.

**Priority Actions:**
1. **Emergency fund**: Route all ₹10,000/month surplus to liquid MF for next 11 months to reach ₹5.88L target
2. **PPF**: Increase annual contribution from ₹35,000 to ₹1,50,000 (pay the ₹1.15L gap via lump sum before March)
3. **NPS**: Start ₹50,000/year NPS Tier 1 to unlock additional ₹15,500 tax saving (30% of ₹50K)
4. **ELSS SIP**: Resume ₹5,000/month ELSS SIP once emergency fund complete (80C top-up)
5. **Term insurance**: ₹1.5Cr cover (15× salary, Mumbai family dependant) — ~₹14,000/yr
6. **Equity planning**: At 35% stake, model exit at ₹0 / ₹5Cr / ₹20Cr / ₹50Cr

**Equity Exit Scenario Table (for ₹50Cr, ₹100Cr, ₹200Cr company exits):**
| Company Exit Valuation | Founder Stake (35%) | After Next Dilution (~25%) | Pre-tax Value | Post LTCG @20% (unlisted) |
|----------------------|--------------------|-----------------------------|---------------|--------------------------|
| ₹10 Cr | ₹3.5 Cr | ₹2.63 Cr | ₹2.63 Cr | ₹2.1 Cr |
| ₹50 Cr | ₹17.5 Cr | ₹13.1 Cr | ₹13.1 Cr | ~₹10.5 Cr |
| ₹200 Cr | ₹70 Cr | ₹52.5 Cr | ₹52.5 Cr | ~₹42 Cr |
| ₹0 (failure) | ₹0 | ₹0 | ₹0 | ₹0 |

> Key insight: At ₹50Cr exit, founder nets ~₹10.5Cr post-tax. That's 7+ years away. Personal savings are the only certain wealth path. Build them NOW regardless of exit expectations.

**Advance Tax Schedule (₹14.4L salary — TDS likely covers this; verify with CA):**
If additional income (consulting, interest > ₹10K): Pay advance tax quarterly.

## Scenario C: The Ex-Corporate Founder (Quit High-Paying Job, Delhi NCR)

**Profile:**
- Age: 40 | City: Gurgaon | Married, 2 children | Parents dependant
- Left ₹45L/year VP role at MNC 10 months ago
- Startup salary: ₹60,000/month (₹7.2L/year; bare minimum to survive)
- Monthly take-home: ~₹55,000 (after TDS)
- Monthly personal burn: ₹1,35,000
  - Rent: ₹60,000 | Home loan EMI (hometown property): ₹18,000 | School fees: ₹15,000 | Food: ₹15,000 | Parents support: ₹12,000 | Insurance & misc: ₹15,000
- Monthly deficit: -₹80,000/month (drawing from savings)
- Cash savings: ₹28,00,000 (from prior job savings + EPF withdrawal)
- Equity: 40% (co-founder, 4-year vesting — 10 months in)
- EPF balance (new startup): ₹0 (startup too small for EPF; was withdrawn from old job)
- Existing investments: ₹12,50,000 in PPF (18 years in — near maturity), ₹8,00,000 in mutual funds (ELSS + flexicap), ₹3,50,000 in FDs
- Debt: Home loan ₹22L outstanding (EMI ₹18,000/month — 7% p.a., 8 years remaining)

**Founder Runway:**
```
Personal Runway = ₹28,00,000 ÷ ₹80,000 (monthly deficit) = 35 months
```
> ✅ Green at 35 months. BUT this is DECLINING fast. If salary doesn't increase in 18 months, runway drops to critical zone.

**EMI-to-Income Ratio:** ₹18,000 ÷ ₹55,000 = 32.7% ⚠️ High — manageable but limits flexibility

**Emergency Fund Assessment:**
- Target: ₹1,35,000 × 6 = ₹8,10,000
- Ring-fence ₹8,10,000 from savings as untouchable emergency fund in liquid MF
- Remaining usable savings: ₹28L − ₹8.1L = ₹19.9L as personal startup runway

**Tax Analysis (Old Regime strongly preferred):**
| Deduction | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Standard Deduction | ₹50,000 |
| 80C: PPF (₹1.5L — PPF nearly matured; reinvest into new PPF cycle) | ₹1,50,000 |
| 80D: Self + family + parents (senior citizens) | ₹25,000 + ₹50,000 = ₹75,000 |
| Home loan interest | ~₹1,30,000/yr (early years; confirm with statement) |
| 80CCD(1B): NPS | ₹50,000 |
| **Total deductions** | **~₹4.55L** |

> With ₹7.2L gross salary and ₹4.55L deductions, taxable income = ~₹2.65L → near-zero tax liability
> Old regime is overwhelmingly better here. Consider increasing NPS to further reduce taxable income.

**Priority Actions:**
1. **Immediate**: Define a personal "salary trigger" milestone — at what company revenue will salary increase to ₹1.35L? Make it explicit in the plan.
2. **Month 1**: Ring-fence ₹8.1L as emergency fund in a separate liquid MF account
3. **Month 1**: Renew PPF for another 5 years (PPF extends in 5-year blocks after 15 years) — ₹1.5L/year
4. **Month 1**: Ensure both parents covered under separate super top-up health plan (₹20L cover, ₹10L deductible, costs ~₹15,000–20,000/yr)
5. **Month 2**: Start ₹5,000/month Nifty 50 SIP from salary — even small SIP preserves compounding habit
6. **Month 3**: Prepay home loan? Only if EMI stress is high AND interest > 7%. At 7%, index fund SIP (12% historical) is mathematically better than prepayment.
7. **Consider**: 1–2 consulting days/month to supplement income — many ex-VPs can earn ₹50,000–1,00,000/month part-time without conflicting with startup

**Retirement Check (at age 40):**
Current corpus: PPF ₹12.5L + Mutual funds ₹8L + FDs ₹3.5L = ₹24L
At 7% real return for 20 years: ₹24L × (1.07)^20 = ~₹92.8L
Monthly retirement need (today's ₹): ₹1,35,000 → ₹1,35,000 × 12 × 25 = ₹4.05Cr target (in today's ₹)
Inflation-adjusted target (6% for 20 years): ₹4.05Cr × (1.06)^20 = ₹12.99Cr

> **Gap**: Current trajectory yields ~₹93L from existing corpus; target is ~₹13Cr. Gap = ~₹12Cr.
> This highlights why salary increase + active investing are not optional — they are urgent.

## Common Calculations Reference

### Emergency Fund Target
```
Emergency Fund = Monthly Personal Burn × 6 months
Minimum Floor = Monthly Personal Burn × 3 months
Vehicle: Liquid Mutual Fund (T+1 liquidity, ~6.5–7% p.a.)
```

### Founder Salary Adequacy Check
```
Minimum Acceptable Salary = Monthly Personal Burn × 12 (annualised)
Target Salary = Monthly Personal Burn × 12 + 20% (savings buffer)
```

### 80C Optimisation Priority
```
1. PPF: ₹1,50,000 (EEE, zero risk) — always fill this first
2. ELSS SIP: Remaining 80C room (market-linked, 3-yr lock-in)
3. Life insurance premium: Only if term policy premium counts (not ULIP/endowment)
```

### SIP Compounding Reference (₹5,000/month at 12% CAGR — Nifty historical)
| Starting Age | Value at 60 |
|-------------|------------|
| 25 | ₹3.5 Cr |
| 30 | ₹1.76 Cr |
| 35 | ₹87 L |
| 40 | ₹41 L |

> At 12% CAGR: Each 5-year delay cuts the final corpus by roughly 50%. Start SIPs immediately.
